# AMFG + CNC Research Brief

## What AMFG appears to do
AMFG positions itself as manufacturing software for quoting and production. The strongest visible themes are:
- estimating and quoting
- auto-quoting
- CAD and drawing driven workflow
- production planning and tracking
- automation and integrations
- digital production control and visibility

## Likely customer pains AMFG solves
- quoting takes too long
- quoting depends too heavily on scarce expert estimators
- pricing and costing logic is inconsistent
- production data is fragmented across systems
- manufacturers need more visibility, control, and automation
- shops want configurable workflows rather than generic one-size-fits-all software

## Website signals that suggest a strong AMFG fit
- request a quote / RFQ flow
- CAD upload or drawing upload
- precision machining or CNC machining language
- mentions of estimating, quoting, machine rates, markups, or costing
- high-mix low-volume work
- subcontract manufacturing or custom parts
- production planning, shop floor control, MES, ERP, workflow, traceability
- industries with compliance or complexity, such as aerospace, medical, defense, industrial

## Website signals that suggest weaker fit
- sheet metal only
- laser / waterjet / stamping / roll-forming only
- additive-only without CNC relevance
- no real production operation
- no RFQ or custom part workflow
- website centered on standard catalog products rather than custom manufacturing

## CNC machining understanding
CNC machining is computer-controlled machining of parts using machine tools. It is highly relevant where precision, repeatability, CAD/CAM workflow, and quoting discipline matter.

### Milling
Milling removes material with a rotating cutter. It is commonly used for complex prismatic parts, pockets, faces, and multi-axis machining.

### Turning
Turning removes material from a rotating workpiece, usually on a lathe. It is common for cylindrical parts, bores, grooves, and threads.

## What a CNC machine shop often looks like
- custom or contract manufacturer
- handles RFQs and engineering drawings
- quotes custom parts
- works from CAD, drawings, or 3D models
- discusses tolerances, materials, machine capability, and lead times
- may highlight 3-axis, 4-axis, 5-axis, turning, mill-turn, prototyping, or production runs

## Important nuance for qualification
Many real machine shops mention adjacent processes like fabrication, welding, or secondary operations. That alone should not force disqualification. Mixed-signal shops should usually go to review unless the non-fit signals dominate.

## Best signals for AI BDR targeting
- explicit CNC machining capability
- request-a-quote workflow
- build-to-print / contract manufacturing language
- aerospace, medical, defense, industrial precision language
- evidence of recurring quoting workload
- signs of operational complexity and multi-step workflow

## Best disqualification evidence to save
- exact mention of sheet metal/fabrication-only specialization
- exact mention of additive-only specialization
- exact mention that they do not take RFQs or do not do custom manufacturing
- exact mention of process focus that conflicts with AMFG CNC target profile
